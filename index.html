Background Information

  1. Affective Computing:

    Affective computing is an interdisciplinary field that studies and develops systems and devices capable of recognizing, understanding, processing, and simulating human emotions. Research in this field dates back to the 1990s.

  2. Early Research:

    Early affective computing research focused on emotion recognition and generation. For instance, recognizing human emotions by analyzing facial expressions, vocal characteristics, and physiological signals, or simulating emotions through emotional speech and expressions.

  3. Existing Technologies:

    Currently, many conversational systems and virtual assistants (like Apple's Siri, Google Assistant, and Amazon's Alexa) have some capability to recognize and respond to emotions. For example, they can analyze users' tone and text content to infer emotional states and respond accordingly.
